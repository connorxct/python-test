**QUESTION 5: (15 points)** (fictitious) A journal published a study on the gender of children in two-child families. The article reported on the results of the Global Health Issues Interview Survey of almost 50,000 two-child families. The table gives the proportion of families with each gender configuration.

Gender Configuration Proportion
Boy-boy (BB) 0.235
Girl-boy (GB) 0.262
Boy-girl (BG) 0.251
Girl-girl (GG) 0.252

Let the random variable X represent the number of girls in a two-child family.

A. (6 points) Find the probability distribution for X.

X=0, X=1, X=2

B. (6 points) Find the expected value for X.

Pr(X=0) = 0.235 Pr(X=1) = 0.262+0.251 = 0.513 Pr(X=2) = 0.252 E(X) = 0.235+0.513+0.252/3 = 0.333 ~ 33.3%

C. (3 points) Provide a real-world interpretation for the expected value you found in Part (B).

The expected value of X calculated in part B means that there is 33.3% chance of having at least 1 girl in a two-child family.
